# Soil health basics

Foundational notes on soil properties relevant to many crops. Always validate with local soil tests.

## pH

- Most agronomic crops perform well near neutral (roughly 6.0–7.0), but optimal range is crop-specific (e.g., blueberries prefer lower pH).
- Lime raises pH; sulfur or acidifying amendments lower it—rates depend on soil buffer capacity and test results.

## Major nutrients

- **Nitrogen (N)** — drives vegetative growth; losses increase with excess water moving through soil.
- **Phosphorus (P)** — important for roots and energy transfer; movement in soil is limited compared to N.
- **Potassium (K)** — supports water regulation and stress tolerance.

## Organic matter

- Organic matter improves water retention, nutrient buffering, and microbial activity.
- Cover crops, compost, and reduced tillage (where appropriate) can build organic matter over time.
