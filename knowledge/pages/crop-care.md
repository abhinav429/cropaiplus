# General crop care

CropAI guidance for day-to-day crop management. Use with local extension advice and field conditions.

## Watering

- Prefer deep, less frequent irrigation over shallow daily sprinkles where appropriate for the crop.
- Morning watering reduces leaf wetness overnight and can lower fungal pressure in many systems.
- Match water to growth stage: establishment often needs consistent moisture; maturity may need dry-down for harvest quality (crop-specific).

## Scouting

- Walk fields regularly; early stress shows in color, leaf angle, or uneven growth before severe damage.
- Note patterns: scattered damage often suggests insects or birds; uniform rows may suggest equipment, fertility, or drainage.

## Seasonal timing

- Align planting windows with your region’s frost dates and the crop’s degree-day needs.
- Rotate crops where possible to break pest and disease cycles and balance nutrient use.
